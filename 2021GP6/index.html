本次演講主題

Deep-learning-based Speech Enhancement with Its Application to Assistive Oral Communications Devices

講員簡介

講員簡介

Yu Tsao received the B.S. and M.S. degrees in electrical engineering from National Taiwan University, Taipei, Taiwan, in 1999 and 2001, respectively, and the Ph.D. degree in electrical and computer engineering from the Georgia Institute of Technology, Atlanta, GA, USA, in 2008. From 2009 to 2011, he was a Researcher with the National Institute of Information and Communications Technology, Tokyo, Japan, where he engaged in research and product development in automatic speech recognition for multilingual speech-to-speech translation. He is currently a Research Fellow (Professor) and Deputy Director with the Research Center for Information Technology Innovation, Academia Sinica, Taipei. His research interests include speech and speaker recognition, acoustic and language modeling, audio coding, and bio-signal processing. He is currently an Associate Editor for the IEEE/ACM Transactions on Audio, Speech, and Language Processing and IEEE Signal Processing Letters and a Distinguished Lecturer of APSIPA. He was the recipient of the Academia Sinica Career Development Award in 2017, the National Innovation Award in 2018, 2019, 2020, Future Tech Breakthrough Award 2019, and the Outstanding Elite Award, Chung Hwa Rotary Educational Foundation 2019–2020.

演講內容簡介

演講內容簡介

Speech enhancement (SE)

Reducing distortions caused by additive and convoluted noises in order to achieving improved human-human and human-machine communication efficacy.

Deep learning based SE

In the this talk, we will review the system architecture and fundamental theories of deep learning based SE approaches.

Recent advances

We will present more recent advances, including end-to-end and goal-driven based SE systems as well as the SE systems with improved architectures and feature extraction procedure.

Applications

We will discuss some applications based on the deep learning SE systems, including impaired speech transformation and noise reduction for assistive hearing and speaking devices.

演講心得

StudentA:聽完這次的演講,知道人工電子耳和助聽器的不同,人工電子耳的功能是讓完全聽不見的人能夠聽聽見聲音,這真是一個真正對人類友幫助的研究. 曹博士也特別介紹人類耳朵的運作是聲音先轉成動能再轉成電能,而電子耳的運作是聲音直接轉成電能,真是受益匪淺. 本次演講擔任結尾問答主持,我自己先問了一個助聽器的問題,沒想到許多同學也陸續提出自己的問題,可能是因為只需開麥克風,不用露臉所以大家都不吝嗇的發言,真是不錯!

StudentB:今天曹博士的演講主要是關於speech enhancement(SE)的方面，基於機器學習中的深度學習在語音強化上的應用，一開始曹博士有先大概描述一下人工智慧、機器學習以及深度學習之間的關係，人工智慧是目標，機器學習是達成目標的方法，而深度學習則是機器學習的一種類型或者說是層數較高的機器學習，然後還有說到神經網路的起源，神經網路就是用電腦去模擬神經系統的運作，在電腦裡設計出類神經元(Analog(累加)以及all-or-none nerve firing(傳遞: 1/0))，以其為單位串聯成神經網路，並點出電腦學習與人類學習得共同性，其中一個就是把錯誤最小化，最後展示了很多se的應用，包括降造,語音轉換,助聽….之類的，其中我最印象深刻的是提取人聲消除雜音的部分，曹博士有提到人類在說話的時候，其實聲音是有結構性的，可以根據結構性提取純人聲，雖然效果沒有比深度學習好，但也是一種方法，這讓我想到不知道能不能用類似的方法在歌曲中提取人聲，把背景音樂(旋律)當成雜訊，只提取清唱的部分，但是曲子本身也是有結構性的，而且歌唱的時候難免會有一些真音假音甚至破音的轉換，不知道這樣會不會有影響，能不能用深度學習的方式來解決?由於今天的主題好像不是音樂，所以我也沒有問這個問題，總之今天的講座讓我學到了很多關於語音相關的知識，曹講員的演講方式也非常的生動活潑，讓我在這次演講中聽而聞之，收穫滿滿。
